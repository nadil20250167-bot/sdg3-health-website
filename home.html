Small Actions. Real Impact. Good Health for Everyone.

SDG is a student project supporting UN Sustainable Development Goal 3 Good Health and Well-Being. We don't just explain the goal we show practical, everyday actions that students, families and communities can take right now to improve physical and mental health.

Move Every Day

Physical inactivity is one of the leading risk factors for preventable illness among students, who often spend long hours sitting in lectures or studying. A short daily walk or stretch break can measurably lower stress and improve concentration.

  • Take a 10-minute walk between study sessions.
  • Use stairs instead of lifts on campus.
  • Join a free university sports club.

Protect Your Mind

Mental health is central to SDG 3, yet it's often overlooked in student life. Recognising early signs of stress or burnout, and normalising conversations about mental well-being, benefits the whole community not just the individual.

  • Talk to someone you trust when overwhelmed.
  • Take regular screen-free breaks.
  • Use your university's free counselling service.

Eat to Thrive

Good nutrition directly supports immune function, energy levels and mood. For students on a budget, small, consistent changes matter more than expensive diets or trends.

  • Add one extra portion of vegetables a day.
  • Batch-cook to avoid relying on takeaways.
  • Stay hydrated carry a reusable water bottle.
